Under the hood

What "one-click connect"
actually means

Every supported source goes through the same three steps before a single record enters the fan graph.

Step 01 · Connect

Credentials in, pipe open

OAuth or service credentials with least-privilege scopes. The connector authenticates against your tool and opens a monitored pipe. No CSV exports or nightly zip files.

OAuth ✓ · scopes: read:purchases, read:members

Step 02 · Map

The tool's schema lands on the graph

Fields from each tool are mapped onto the fan graph: events, identifiers, and attributes. A "purchase" means the same thing whether it came from ticketing or the webshop.

stripe.charge → purchase · seat · value · fan id

Step 03 · Govern

Consent checked before anything moves

Residency and consent rules are enforced at the pipe itself. Data that isn't allowed to move stays put, and every record that does move carries its consent state with it.

consent: email ✓ ads ✗ · residency: in-region ✓

Why it matters

Logos are easy.
Pipes are hard.

Anyone can print a grid of logos. What matters is what Fanfinity reliably collects from each source: real fan events and attributes, normalized and governed before they touch the graph.
