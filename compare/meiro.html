A fair read

Two composable CDPs, two different centers of gravity

Meiro and Fanfinity agree on the hard parts of a modern data platform: collect first-party signals, resolve identity in real time, activate across channels, and keep the infrastructure under the customer's control. Meiro does this exceptionally well across banking, retail, media, and iGaming as a general horizontal CDP. We are not going to pretend otherwise.

Our angle is narrower on purpose. Fanfinity is built around one buyer, the fan, for sports and live entertainment, resolved and stored in-Kingdom under PDPL, with sponsorship and exposure-to-outcome proof as first-class concerns rather than a downstream report. Where a general CDP has to fit every industry, we optimize for one.

Meiro's strength is breadth: one architecture that serves many industries, warehouse-composable, deployable anywhere. Fanfinity trades breadth for depth in one domain. Neither posture is better in the abstract; the right one depends on whether your organizing entity is a customer or a fan.

Posture comparison

Both are composable and deployable. The differences are focus and residency, not a feature gap.

Meiro observations reflect its published positioning as a horizontal CDP. Fanfinity rows marked "(to confirm)" are posture hypotheses, not stated facts.

When Meiro is the better fit

We will tell you when to pick them

If you need a general horizontal CDP spanning banking, retail, and e-commerce with warehouse-native composability across many teams, Meiro is a strong, credible platform and worth a serious look. Fanfinity earns its place when the buyer is fans, the moment is live, the proof owed is to sponsors, and the data has to stay in-Kingdom. That is the slice we are built for, and the honesty rule cuts both ways.
