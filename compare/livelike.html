The honest read

Where the two platforms actually differ

On the engagement and loyalty surface (polls, quizzes, predictions, rewards, leaderboards, streaks, tiers) the two are broadly matched. We model our engagement feature set on the incumbent, so we will not claim capabilities a hosted suite lacks. The real question a serious evaluator asks is different: who owns the fan data, where does it live, and does the cost grow every time a fan engages. That is where our posture diverges.

One honesty note up front: our data and identity layer is live today; the live-engagement layer is a design Preview, not a shipped product. LiveLike's engagement suite is a mature, delivered product. We say so plainly.

Most engagement suites hold the fan relationship inside their own platform. Fanfinity keeps the fan graph consented, first-party, and in-region — the layer you own — and treats live engagement as one activation on top of it. That is the right fit for a rights-holder or broadcaster who has to answer procurement and residency questions before they think about polls and quizzes.

Where LiveLike is genuinely strong

We do not oversell against a mature suite

LiveLike ships a broad, delivered engagement and loyalty suite with real live-media depth and its own production tooling. If you need an out-of-the-box engagement app today, that is a genuine strength and we will say so. Our engagement layer is a design Preview. Where we ask to be measured is ownership, residency, and a cost model that does not punish success, and each of those is a posture we hold ourselves to, labeled until proven.
