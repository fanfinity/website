The honest read

The best raw events vs a graph ready to act on

Snowplow's reputation is earned: schema-validated, richly structured behavioral events, collected at serious scale, delivered into infrastructure you control. Data teams that want maximum-quality raw material for modeling, analytics, and machine learning choose it for good reasons, and its private-cloud deployment story is genuinely strong.

Fanfinity optimizes for a different buyer. A club, broadcaster, or brand rarely wants raw event tables; it wants to know who the fan is, reach them while the moment is live, and prove to a sponsor what the exposure drove. So the platform ships with identity resolution, activation, and measurement already connected, resolved to one consented profile, in-region under PDPL.

Put simply: with event infrastructure, identity, activation, and attribution are projects your team builds downstream. With Fanfinity, they are the product, and the business team can operate them without standing up a modeling practice first.

Posture comparison

Both take data quality and ownership seriously. The decision sits in how much of the loop arrives built.

Sponsorship & exposure-to-outcome proof Built to tie sponsored exposure to resolved outcomes. Not a focus; attribution is something your team models on the data.

Snowplow observations reflect its published positioning as behavioral data infrastructure. Fanfinity rows describe our intended posture, not independently verified benchmarks.

When Snowplow is the better fit

The honest boundary

If you run a mature data organization that wants maximum-fidelity behavioral events as raw material, and you have the team to model identity, activation, and attribution on top, Snowplow is a serious, well-earned choice. Fanfinity earns its place when the buyer is a fan business rather than a data platform team: one resolved graph, live activation, sponsor-grade proof, and in-Kingdom residency, without the modeling practice as a prerequisite.
