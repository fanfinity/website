The honest read

An activation layer vs a full data stack

Hightouch does one job well: it takes customer data your team has already collected, cleaned, and modeled in your own warehouse, and it routes that data to the destinations you activate against. If you have a mature warehouse and a data team that owns collection, identity, and modeling, that focus is a real strength.

Fanfinity works further up the pipeline. We collect first-party signals, resolve identity in real time into one consented fan graph, and then activate it, without assuming you already have a warehouse team standing behind it. The whole graph is resolved and stored in-Kingdom under PDPL. Same activation job at the end; a different amount of the stack we own for you.

Hightouch's model is elegant when the hard work is already done: a warehouse team owns collection, cleansing, and identity, and Hightouch turns that prepared data into activation. Fanfinity is built for teams that want the fan graph resolved for them, in real time, and kept in-region, without first standing up a full warehouse practice.

Posture comparison

Both activate first-party data. The decision sits in what happens before activation, and where the data lives.

When Hightouch is the better fit

The honest boundary

If you already run a mature warehouse with a data team that owns collection, identity, and modeling, and you want a focused, well-built layer to activate that data, Hightouch is a strong, credible choice. Fanfinity earns its place when you want collection and real-time identity resolution handled for you, when the data has to stay in-Kingdom, and when the outcome you owe is to fans and sponsors rather than a synced destination.
