## 1. Who we are selling to (audience frame)

Fanfinity is a fan-engagement and loyalty platform. The buyers are **business and product leaders** at three vertical types. We lead with **outcomes and use cases**, not API internals (the developer track is secondary).

| Vertical | What they own | Core unit of value they care about |
|---|---|---|
| **Sports leagues & teams** | The fan relationship across a season and across competitions | Season-long retention, app return visits, sponsor-able moments |
| **Broadcasters & media** | Audience attention during live and on-demand content | Watch time / second-screen engagement, content performance, ad/sponsor inventory |
| **Brands** (and rights-holders running campaigns) | Campaign activation and loyalty | Activation, repeat engagement, measurable campaign outcomes |

These map onto Fanfinity's documented capabilities for Widgets, Quests, Streaks, Rewards, Leaderboards, Status Tiers, Sponsorship, and Analytics. **(High confidence on capability mapping; Medium on which vertical buys which capability first — to confirm.)**

> Note: specific in-app engagement metrics (e.g., DAU/MAU lift, watch-time lift) are **not** established in the source and must not be presented as Fanfinity outcomes. The Analytics capability *measures* DAU/WAU/MAU and engagement; it does not, on its own, prove a lift figure. Treat any numeric outcome as **TBD / to confirm**.

### Persona B — "The Audience / Second-Screen Lead" (Broadcaster / media)

## 4. JTBD Four Forces (Push / Pull / Habit / Anxiety)

> The Four Forces model the *switch decision*: forces driving change vs. forces resisting it. **All entries are hypotheses to validate in discovery interviews** — they are framed from the product/category, not from observed buyer behavior. (Low/Medium confidence throughout.)

### Forces *toward* change (drive a switch / purchase)

**PUSH — what's wrong with the current situation**
- Off-season / inter-content engagement collapses; passive audiences bounce.
- Sponsors and boards demand engagement proof the team can't produce.
- Every season/campaign is a slow, expensive bespoke build.
- Spoiler complaints undermine the live experience.
- Audiences are drifting to social platforms the property doesn't own or measure.

**PULL — what's attractive about Fanfinity** *(described as Fanfinity's own capabilities)*
- A broad, expanding interactive widget library (polls, image slider, trivia/quiz, predictions, number prediction, cheer meter, alerts, ask, social embeds) with real-time delivery so widgets appear live without polling.
- A full gamification/loyalty stack (Quests, Streaks, Rewards, Leaderboards, Badges, Status Tiers) under one platform, connected through a single fan profile.
- Sponsorship + per-sponsor / per-widget theming to turn engagement into monetizable, branded inventory.
- Built-in spoiler prevention for live/sports (client-side, playback-synced).
- Built-in analytics (Standard reports for all; Visual Analytics and the Analytics API as add-ons) to measure performance.
- Unified, cross-platform fan identity that persists across sessions, installs, and devices.
- **Differentiation wedges (decided 2026-06-01):**
  - *Connected, owned all-in-one stack* — **grounded today:** engagement→loyalty→rewards→identity→analytics line up by design under one fan profile. **Lead here.**
  - *First-party data ownership* — chosen wedge **(to confirm — needs a defined deployment/data-residency model; spec'd delivery uses hosted PubNub).**
  - *Engagement-friendly pricing* — chosen wedge: incumbents bill per profile / metered MAU; Fanfinity's model is **TBD** and must be defined this way before claiming.
  - *Reliability / performance* — **deprioritized, not a lead** (unbenchmarked; numbers come from a separate analytics project).

### Forces *against* change (resist a switch / purchase)

**HABIT / INERTIA — comfort with the present**
- An incumbent engagement vendor already integrated and "good enough."
- In-house tooling and agency relationships already in place.
- Reliance on social-platform engagement, even if unowned/unmeasured.
- "We'll deal with off-season engagement later."

**ANXIETY — fear about the new solution**
- Migration/switching cost and risk from an existing vendor.
- Will it actually move retention, or is it just widgets?
- Can a non-engineering (marketing/product) team operate it? *(to confirm)*
- Integration effort across iOS / Android / Web and existing players; spoiler-sync prerequisites (timecode metadata + compatible player; HLS `EXT-X-PROGRAM-DATE-TIME` for chat sync).
- Data residency, security, and where fan data lives (TBD — needs a clear, confirmable answer).
- Pricing/packaging unknowns, including which analytics tiers are add-ons (Visual Analytics and the Analytics API are documented as add-ons; Fanfinity's packaging is **to confirm**).

> **Capability-gap caution (do not claim parity):** Competing suites may offer adjacent capabilities — e.g., chat, comments, arcade mini-games, an AI CMS assistant, and an MCP server — that the Fanfinity source epics do **not** establish. Do not imply Fanfinity covers these; treat as **TBD / to confirm**.

**Messaging implication (hypothesis):** To win the switch, Fanfinity should amplify PUSH (cost of passive/decaying engagement, unmonetized inventory, spoiler risk) and PULL (one integrated, identity-connected stack), while actively *reducing ANXIETY* — proof of retention impact, low-/no-code operability for business teams, a clear migration path, and a confirmable data-ownership and pricing story. **None of these reducers should be asserted as fact until confirmed.**
