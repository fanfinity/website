## Part A — Value Pillars

Six outcome-oriented pillars. Each maps real Fanfinity capabilities to the business result a buyer is trying to buy.

### 1. Turn watching into playing
**Promise:** Make any live moment interactive, so passive viewers become active participants in real time.

**What backs it (Fanfinity capabilities):**
- Interactive **Widgets** library — polls, quizzes, predictions, number predictions, cheer meters, alerts, ask, image sliders, and social embeds
- Real-time delivery to subscribed apps, so widgets appear the instant a key moment happens (no client polling of the program timeline)
- Three presentation modes: popup (timed, one at a time), timeline (browsable feed), and embedded (on a web page)
- Build-by-hand or fully automated, scheduled-in-advance publishing via the two-step create-then-publish lifecycle
- **Programs** that organize content (a stream, an episode, a post) and scope every interactive moment to the right game, show, or segment

**Business outcome it drives:** Higher watch time and second-screen engagement; fans stay inside the app longer, creating interactive inventory that can be measured and monetized.

---

### 2. Build habits that last a season
**Promise:** Convert one-off viewers into fans who come back daily, weekly, and all season long.

**What backs it (Fanfinity capabilities):**
- **Streaks** — periodic (daily/weekly/monthly, timezone-aware) and consecutive-action, with milestone reward checkpoints and streak freezing
- **Quests** — multi-step challenges for onboarding checklists, feature tours, and one-time campaigns, with per-user progress tracking
- **Badges** — earnable and awardable achievements that attach permanently to a fan's profile
- **Status Tiers** — progressive VIP/loyalty levels (e.g. Gold, Platinum) that unlock accumulating benefits
- Built-in **A/B testing** on quests, with server-side consistent assignment and per-variant metrics, to learn what actually motivates fans

**Business outcome it drives:** Retention and repeat engagement — the recurring return-visit metric leagues, broadcasters, and sponsors care about, plus a way to test and improve activation.

---

### 3. Reward the behavior you value most
**Promise:** Run a full points-and-rewards economy that incentivizes exactly the actions that matter to your business.

**What backs it (Fanfinity capabilities):**
- **Rewards** engine — reward items as score, XP, or collectibles; reward tables mapping actions to amounts; capping and rate limiting
- Built-in earning actions for widget interactions (votes, quiz answers, predictions, Ask replies) plus custom actions like "invited a friend"
- Full economy: check, credit, debit, and **transfer balances** between users (gifting/tipping), with transaction history
- **Leaderboards** that update automatically from rewards earned in linked programs, supporting all-time, seasonal, and single-event rankings, with curated **leaderboard views** for segments
- **Reactions** and **Flair** layers that add lightweight, expressive engagement and consistent identity/status signals across any content

**Business outcome it drives:** Deeper fan investment and time spent; a loyalty layer you can tune to reward the behaviors tied to your revenue and engagement goals.

---

### 4. Own your fan relationship
**Promise:** A single, persistent fan identity that ties every interaction, reward, and score to one profile — across sessions, devices, and platforms.

**What backs it (Fanfinity capabilities):**
- **Profiles & Identity** — one profile per user collecting all activity, rewards, and scores; persistent and anonymous-then-linked usage
- Profiles **enhance, not replace** your existing user records, with a custom ID for two-way mapping to your own user IDs
- Built-in **Analytics** — standard pre-built reports at application, program, widget, quest, and quest-task levels; per-widget impressions, unique reach, and engagement counts
- Visual Analytics dashboards (DAU/WAU/MAU, widget engagement, quest funnels, chat volume) and a programmatic Analytics API *(documented as an add-on; commercial packaging TBD / to confirm)*
- Safety and community controls: profile **blocking** (system-enforced across comments, chat, and social graph) and a directed **social graph** (e.g. follow)

**Business outcome it drives:** First-party engagement data and a unified audience view that follows the fan everywhere — supporting retention strategy, content decisions, and audience analytics you control.

---

### 5. Make your inventory sponsorable
**Promise:** Turn fan-engagement moments into branded, sponsor-ready inventory you can package and sell.

**What backs it (Fanfinity capabilities):**
- **Sponsorship** — attach a sponsor's name, logo/banner, and brand color to programs, quests, and other resources, surfaced to apps via API/SDK
- Stable sponsor identity reused everywhere a sponsor appears
- **Custom Themes** — per-widget unique theming, so an experience can be re-skinned per sponsor (colors, borders, corner radii, fonts), saved in a standard format and reused across iOS, Android, and Web
- **Redemption Keys** for controlled promotions, giveaways, and merch/ticket fulfillment without double-redemption
- Engagement Analytics to back sponsorship reporting with concrete reach and engagement numbers

**Business outcome it drives:** New sponsorship revenue — quests, widgets, and programs become measurable, brandable placements rights-holders and brands can attach value to.

*(Note: sponsor create/update/delete is UI-managed in the source; API-level sponsor management is TBD / to confirm. Theming coverage note: the common theme format does not yet cover Cheer Meters or Rich Text, which require custom code per the source.)*

---

### 6. Open new monetization paths
**Promise:** Give fans real reasons to spend — and give your business new ways to earn beyond ads and subscriptions.

**What backs it (Fanfinity capabilities):**
- **Rewards** redemption, including a Reward Store catalog and monetization-enabled cash-out (Prizeout) for eligible items
- Gifting and tipping via balance transfers between fans
- **Status Tiers** with rewardable benefits that create premium membership and VIP perks
- Exclusive, profile-level **Reaction packs** that can be granted via purchases, rewards, or membership tiers
- **Redemption Keys** for exclusive virtual or physical goods

**Business outcome it drives:** Monetization through rewards redemption, premium tiers, and exclusive perks — turning engagement into a revenue line, not just a cost center.

### Value-prop statements

Use these as section headers, ad copy, or sales talking points. Each pairs a buyer outcome with grounded capability.

1. **Make every live moment playable.** Push polls, predictions, and quizzes the instant a key moment happens, and watch passive viewers turn into active participants who stay in your app longer.

2. **Keep fans coming back all season.** Streaks, quests, badges, and status tiers build the daily and weekly habits that drive retention — not just one-time spikes around big games.

3. **Reward what matters to your business.** A full points-and-rewards economy lets you incentivize the exact behaviors you value, then rank fans on leaderboards that update automatically.

4. **Own your fan data.** One persistent profile ties every interaction, reward, and score to a single fan across devices and platforms — enhancing your existing user records, not replacing them, with built-in engagement analytics.

5. **Sell your engagement as inventory.** Brand quests, widgets, and programs to sponsors, re-skin experiences per partner, and back it all with reach and engagement numbers.

6. **Turn engagement into revenue.** Reward stores, cash-out, gifting, premium tiers, and exclusive perks open monetization paths beyond ads and subscriptions.

### Differentiation (decided 2026-06-01 — the wedges we lead on)

Decision: lead on **three wedges**. Only the first is a present fact; the other two are **(to confirm)** and must be substantiated before any public claim. The competitor is named only here, in internal notes — never in Fanfinity's own capability copy.

- **① Connected all-in-one stack — LEAD, grounded today:** engagement → loyalty → rewards → identity → analytics line up *by design, not by integration* (rewards auto-feed leaderboards, trip badge/tier thresholds, accrue to one profile). A present fact per the specs, not a hypothesis.
- **② First-party data ownership — chosen wedge (to confirm):** one fan identity the customer owns. The identity model is grounded; the *ownership/deployment* angle needs a defined deployment/data-residency story (spec'd delivery uses hosted PubNub — reconcile first).
- **③ Engagement-friendly pricing — chosen wedge (to confirm):** a model that doesn't grow per active user like the competitor's metered-MAU billing. Needs Fanfinity pricing to actually be defined this way (currently TBD).
- **Deprioritized — NOT a lead:** performance/reliability under spikes. The competitor publishes no throughput/latency numbers, but Fanfinity's figures (≈1000 req/s, sub-200ms) come from a *separate analytics-microservice project*, are unbenchmarked, and are **not product facts**. Discovery angle only; never a claim unless benchmarked.
- **Out of scope (do NOT claim parity or advantage):** AI CMS assistant / MCP, chat/comments, arcade mini-games — competitor capabilities, not Fanfinity epics.
