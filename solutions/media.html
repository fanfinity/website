02

The advertising use case

Sell first-party audiences directly, when the cookie is gone

Third-party cookies underwrote most programmatic revenue, and they are leaving. For a publisher, the compensating asset is a consented first-party audience packaged as inventory you sell yourself. Fanfinity resolves that audience on your properties and turns it into addressable segments buyers can transact against, governed in-Kingdom so residency never stalls the deal.

  • {% include icon.html name="check" class="h-4 w-4" %} Build addressable segments from consented first-party behavior, with no third-party cookie in the path.
  • {% include icon.html name="check" class="h-4 w-4" %} Sell those segments to advertisers directly, as inventory you own rather than reach you rent.
  • {% include icon.html name="check" class="h-4 w-4" %} Keep every audience profile governed in-Kingdom, so a data residency clause never blocks a media deal.
